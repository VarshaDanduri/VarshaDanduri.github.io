Aura

Biosignals · Sentence Transformers · k-NN · UMAP · Generative Art

An artist created 500 brushstrokes across five emotional categories (joy, calm, neutral, sad, anger), each paired with a written description tagging its emotional intent. We encode these descriptions using a pretrained sentence-transformer (all-MiniLM-L6-v2), producing 384-dimensional embeddings that preserve the artist's affective intent as a learned representation.

A k-nearest-neighbors classifier maps real-time biosignals onto this space, and UMAP projects it into 2D canvas coordinates. Each stroke on the painting is chosen by matching the user's physiological-emotional state to an artist's felt interpretation of that same emotion. The painting is a translation of biosignal data through the artist's emotional vocabulary, not stochastic generation.
